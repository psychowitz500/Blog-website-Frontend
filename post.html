Vaibhav Suryavanshi, Rajasthan Royals' 14-year-old batting prodigy, made history in April 2025. Against Gujarat Titans, at just 14 years and 32 days, he became the youngest player ever to score an IPL half-century, reaching the mark in a record 17 balls for the season. He spectacularly converted this into a 35-ball century (scoring 101 runs), making him the youngest T20 centurion globally and holder of the second-fastest IPL hundred. The left-hander from Bihar, also the youngest IPL debutant, hit 11 sixes, instantly becoming one of cricket's most talked-about emerging talents.
